# English Static Site Article Writing Standard

Last updated: 2026-07-03

This standard is based on the Obsidian SEO/GEO writing framework and the current static-site publishing problems.

## Non-negotiable baseline

- Formal article pages must have at least 1,000 visible English words.
- The article must answer the main query in the first section, but the answer cannot read like a dictionary entry only.
- Every article needs at least four H2 sections, one FAQ section, and clear related-page routing.
- Paragraphs should stay short. A paragraph over 130 words is a warning unless it is a table note or legal copy.
- Do not publish pages that only pass a score report while reading like filler.

## Required human-readable structure

1. Answer-first opening
   - Start with the practical answer.
   - Add one real reader scenario within the first 2-3 paragraphs.
   - Good patterns: "If you are choosing...", "When you compare...", "For a beginner...", "Before buying...".

2. Context section
   - Explain what the topic means and where the cultural idea comes from.
   - Keep claims bounded. Avoid promises about luck, personality, health, wealth, or guaranteed outcomes.

3. Practical judgment section
   - Add concrete checks, examples, or a comparison table.
   - Include at least one common mistake and one quality/risk signal.

4. Reader path section
   - Tell different readers what to do next: beginner, buyer, learner, gift giver, researcher, parent, or culture-focused reader.
   - Link to the next relevant guide naturally.

5. FAQ
   - Use direct answers that AI can extract.
   - Answers should be useful to humans first, then easy for AI to cite.

## Tone rules

- Write like a knowledgeable guide, not a paper, not a template, and not an internal audit note.
- Use short paragraphs and varied transitions.
- Prefer concrete examples over abstract claims.
- Add judgment sentences: explain why one choice is safer, easier, more practical, or more culturally responsible.
- Keep cultural language respectful and grounded.

## Banned patterns

Do not use these unless quoted as an example of bad writing:

- "In this article..."
- "This comprehensive guide..."
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "It is important to note..."
- "In conclusion..."
- "Overall,..."
- "For advertising review..."
- "For publishing quality control..."
- "For search quality..."
- "This page should help readers..."
- "A useful guide should..."

Also avoid internal phrases such as "this page works as a future monetization bridge" in public article copy. Product or affiliate intent can exist, but the reader should see practical buying advice, not our internal site plan.

## GEO requirements without making the article stiff

- Use short extractable definitions near the top.
- Use tables when comparison helps the user.
- Use FAQ answers with clear subject nouns.
- Add source or evidence language when discussing history, origin, or cultural claims.
- Keep AI-friendly structure, but never sacrifice readability just to fill a checklist.

## Pre-publish checklist

- [ ] 1,000+ visible words for a formal article.
- [ ] First section answers the query and includes a reader scenario.
- [ ] At least four H2 sections.
- [ ] At least one concrete example or comparison.
- [ ] At least one common mistake or risk check.
- [ ] At least one natural next-step internal link group.
- [ ] FAQ present and not duplicated awkwardly.
- [ ] No banned AI/template/internal-audit phrases.
- [ ] Paragraphs are short and readable.
- [ ] The article sounds useful to a real visitor, not written to satisfy a script.
